ABOUT ME

I grew up during the brink of the digital information age from the 1990s to the 2000s. During this era, I enjoyed reading, writing, drawing and surfing the Internet. Waiting for the Internet connection to dial-up as a kid was totally worth the hours of online games and chatrooms I had access to. Now, as an adult, my love of art, literature, and the Internet has not waned. In fact, now that technology's progression has created a more efficient and expedient experience on the World Wide Web, I want to be more than a passive Internet user. I want to be a part of the development of emergent web technology that makes the Internet the revolutionary medium of collaboration and communication it is today. I want to work with a company that develops web-based content and applications that are efficient, engaging, relevant, and meaningful to users of various backgrounds. I also aim to use web development to supplement my experience in education as a designer of online education programs and as a teacher of client-side and server-side programming languages.
